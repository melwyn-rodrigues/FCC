The Early Years

  • Bob Marley is born in Nine Miles, Jamaica, to Norval and Cedella Marley.
  • Family moves to a ghetto in Trench Town, in the city of Kingston. Later in life, Bob dedicated the song 'Trench Town' to this part of his life.
  • 17 year-old Bob Marley records his first song 'Judge Not'. Gets paid 20 pounds - name is mispelled on the cover as "Bob Morley". But the song becomes a local hit.

Bob Marley & the Wailers

  • Bob Marley teams up with Peter Tosh and Neville (Bunny Wailer) Livingston and form the group 'The Wailing Wailers'.
  • 1964 - The Wailing Wailers top the Jamaican singles charts for the first time with the song 'Simmer Down'.
  • 1974 - Peter Tosh & Bunny Wailer part from 'The Wailers' group.

The Legacy

  • 1994 - Bob Marley is inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • 2000-2001 Bob Marley is awarded the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award.
  • 2004 - Rolling Stones Magazine ranks Bob Marley number 11 on their list of the 100 Greatest Artists of all time.
